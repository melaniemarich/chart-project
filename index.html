Beyond the Pandemic: Public Transit's Slow But Steady Recovery

Nearly Four Years Since The Onset of Covid-19, Public Transit Ridership Recovers Slowly

By Melanie Marich | November 20, 2023

Source: Pexels

Ridership in U.S. transit agencies has returned to 80% of pre-pandemic levels but shifted distributions, presenting a crisis for major transit agencies nationwide. Many of the largest agencies in the country are in a fiscal crisis due to plummeting fare revenue and the imminent end of federal relief funds. The Urban Institute released a report this month about this fiscal cliff. While the report suggests diversifying funding sources, some agencies like Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority are increasing service despite facing a $750 million deficit.

Despite challenges, some systems thrive with stable funding, emphasizing the broader benefits of mass transit. Researchers at the Urban Institute remain cautiously optimistic about overcoming financial hurdles, citing success stories like Seattle's increased ridership from expanded services.
